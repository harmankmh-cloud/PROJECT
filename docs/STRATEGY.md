# Strategy — competitive position

Solo founder wedge: **price, focus, Canada/local fit** — not head-on vs incumbents.

## Portfolio summary

| Product | Stage | Moat | Biggest gap |
|---------|-------|------|-------------|
| **RateLocal** | Shipped, cheapest in class | $39 vs $400 Podium | Feature breadth |
| **GreetQ** | Shipped, paying-ready | PIPEDA/CASL + voice bundle | Brand, integrations |
| **ServeLocal** | Early marketplace | BC SEO, no per-lead fee | Pro/homeowner density |

## RateLocal

**Position:** QR/link → Google reviews with AI drafts. **$39/mo Pro.**

**Wins vs:** Podium, Birdeye, NiceJob on price for single-location Google focus.

**Loses vs:** Unified SMS + reviews + payments inbox (Podium's pitch).

**Priority:** Review responses + analytics shipped; 10 BC case studies; upsell path to GreetQ.

## GreetQ

**Position:** AI phone receptionist for BC SMBs. **$79–$399/mo.**

**Wins vs:** Smith.ai/Ruby on price; US tools on Canada compliance story.

**Loses vs:** Brand (Smith.ai 5k+ customers), integration catalog (143+).

**Priority:** 10 named customers + 3 video testimonials; Zapier live; French for QC.

## ServeLocal

**Position:** BC trades directory. **Free listing + $29/mo Featured.**

**Wins vs:** Thumbtack pay-per-lead for budget-conscious Fraser Valley pros.

**Loses vs:** HomeStars density, Google local search.

**Priority:** 50 verified pros in **Abbotsford + Langley** only — not Canada-wide yet.

## Upsell path

**RateLocal → GreetQ:** "You fixed reviews; now fix missed calls."

## Canada wedge

US tools ignore PIPEDA/CASL. Canadian alternatives (Jobbr, HomeStars) don't combine voice + reviews in one stack.
